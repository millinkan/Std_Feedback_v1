About Eschen Chess Club

Our story

Eschen lies in Liechtenstein’s Unterland, north of Vaduz along the Alpine Rhine. Like many Rhein‑Valley communities here, local life runs through municipalities, sports clubs (Vereine), and school halls that double as meeting rooms — a familiar pattern across the Principality’s small towns.

Eschen Chess Club traces its origins to informal Vereinsabend games in the 1990s, when neighbours began meeting after work with boards borrowed from schools and the Gemeinschaftsraum. What started as a handful of clocks and score sheets grew into structured internal leagues mixing rapid rounds on weeknights and longer classical evenings before holidays.

Today the club anchors two rhythms: competitive adults who sharpen openings for tournaments in neighbouring Switzerland and Vorarlberg, and a youth strand held Sunday mornings near Schulhaus Eschen, where juniors learn notation and etiquette before graded play. Rated events elsewhere in the Principality typically follow handbook time‑control norms; organisers keep the same conventions for Eschen‑hosted opens so travellers know what to expect.

About this website
